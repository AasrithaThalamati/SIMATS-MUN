About the Conference

A Premier Diplomatic Simulation Platform

SIMATS Model United Nations 2026 is a distinguished academic conference hosted by Saveetha University, SIMATS Engineering — a premier platform where school and university students converge to deliberate on pressing global issues through the lens of international diplomacy.

Structured in the tradition of the United Nations, SIMATS MUN challenges delegates to step into the shoes of world diplomats — researching their assigned nations, engaging in structured debate, negotiating alliances, and collaborating to craft policy solutions to real-world problems.

Across multiple distinguished committees, delegates debate agenda topics, move through moderated and unmoderated caucuses, author draft resolutions, and ultimately vote on actionable policy frameworks — mirroring the mechanics of actual UN proceedings with rigour and purpose.

How Model United Nations Works

A Step-by-Step Guide Through the Process

01

Registration & Country Allocation

Delegates register for their preferred committee. The Secretariat then assigns each delegate a country position, ensuring balanced regional and political representation across all forums.

02

Research & Position Papers

Delegates conduct in-depth research on their assigned country's stance on the committee's agenda topics. A position paper outlining the nation's official policy and proposed approach is prepared prior to the conference.

03

Committee Sessions & Structured Debate

The conference opens with formal committee sessions chaired by the Executive Board. Delegates deliver formal speeches, challenge opposing positions, and participate in structured diplomatic debate under parliamentary procedure.

04

Moderated & Unmoderated Caucuses

Moderated caucuses allow delegates to raise specific sub-topics for focused debate. Unmoderated caucuses provide informal negotiation time — delegates huddle, bargain, and build blocs that will shape the final resolution.

05

Draft Resolution Writing

Delegate blocs collaborate to author draft resolutions — formal documents containing preambulatory clauses (context) and operative clauses (proposed solutions). These are submitted to the Chair for consideration on the floor.

06

Voting Procedure

Draft resolutions are debated and amended on the floor before proceeding to a formal vote. Delegates vote in favour, against, or abstain, mirroring the procedural norms of actual United Nations assemblies.

07

Awards & Recognition

Outstanding delegates are recognised with prestigious awards including Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, Honourable Mention, and Best Position Paper, presented during the Closing Ceremony.

Rules & Code of Conduct

Upholding the Standards of Diplomatic Excellence

Traditional & Formal Attire

All delegates are required to dress in traditional cultural attire or Western business formal throughout all conference sessions and ceremonies. Both are equally honoured — wear what best represents you.

Diplomatic Language

Delegates must address one another respectfully using appropriate diplomatic terminology. All speeches and interactions must remain constructive, courteous, and free from personal attacks.

Respectful Engagement

Mutual respect across all cultural, national, religious, and ideological differences is mandatory. Discriminatory, offensive, or disruptive conduct will not be tolerated under any circumstances.

Academic Integrity

Delegates must present original research and arguments. Plagiarism, fabrication of data, or misrepresentation of country positions constitutes a breach of conduct and may result in disqualification.

Device Policy

Personal electronic devices may be used solely for research and note-taking during sessions. Unauthorised recording, social media posting, or device misuse during formal proceedings is prohibited.

Executive Board Authority

The ruling of the Executive Board Chairs is final and binding in all procedural matters. Delegates are expected to comply with Chair directives promptly and without dispute.

Organising Secretariat

Leadership, Vision & Operational Excellence

The SIMATS MUN 2026 Organising Secretariat comprises a dedicated team of student leaders and faculty mentors who ensure every dimension of the conference meets the highest standards of academic rigour and operational excellence.

SG

Secretary-General

The highest executive authority of the conference, the Secretary-General provides strategic leadership, presides over plenary sessions, and upholds the overall vision of SIMATS MUN 2026.

DG

Director-General

Working alongside the Secretary-General, the Director-General oversees policy frameworks, committee operations, and ensures procedural consistency across all forums.

DSG

Deputy Secretary-General

The Deputy Secretary-General supports the executive office, manages inter-committee coordination, and serves as a direct liaison between Secretariat leadership and committee chairs.

USG

Under-Secretary-Generals

Each committee is overseen by a designated USG who manages the Executive Board, mentors committee chairs, and ensures that debate quality and procedural standards are maintained throughout.

FC

Faculty Coordinator

The Faculty Coordinator provides institutional guidance and academic oversight, acting as the bridge between the student Secretariat and Saveetha University's administration and faculty.

CET

Core Executive Team

A capable team of operations, logistics, communications, and design professionals who ensure every aspect of the conference — from registration to the closing ceremony — runs flawlessly.
