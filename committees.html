01
UNHRC

United Nations Human Rights Council

Agenda Topic

Addressing Systemic Violations of Civil Liberties and Accountability Mechanisms in Conflict Zones

The United Nations Human Rights Council stands as one of the most morally imperative forums in the international system, dedicated to the promotion and protection of fundamental human rights across the globe. Delegates representing member nations will deliberate on the deepening crises of civil liberties, extrajudicial detentions, humanitarian abuses, and the erosion of rights-based frameworks in conflict-affected regions.

This committee demands that delegates engage deeply with international humanitarian law, the Geneva Conventions, and the UN Charter's human rights provisions while constructing balanced, enforceable, and politically viable resolutions. UNHRC at SIMATS MUN 2026 challenges delegates to navigate the tension between state sovereignty and universal human rights obligations — a debate that lies at the very heart of modern international relations.

Human Rights International Law Humanitarian Policy Accountability Civil Liberties

02
UNGA

United Nations General Assembly

Agenda Topic

Reforming the Architecture of Global Governance for Sustainable Peace and Inclusive Multilateralism

The United Nations General Assembly is the preeminent global forum for multilateral dialogue, bringing together representatives of all member states to deliberate on matters of international peace, collective security, sustainable development, and the reform of global governance institutions. As the most universally representative body in the UN system, the General Assembly holds unparalleled legitimacy in shaping international norms and policy frameworks.

At SIMATS MUN 2026, UNGA provides delegates — especially those new to Model United Nations — with an ideal environment to develop the foundational skills of formal diplomacy: structured speechmaking, bloc formation, amendment procedures, and resolution drafting. Delegates will engage with urgent questions of multilateral cooperation, the reform of international institutions, and the pursuit of a more equitable global order.

International Peace Global Governance Sustainable Development Multilateralism UN Reform

03
ECOFIN

Economic and Financial Affairs Council

Agenda Topic

Restructuring Global Debt Architecture and Financing Sustainable Development in Emerging Economies

The Economic and Financial Affairs Council is the premier forum for deliberation on the complex, interconnected challenges of global financial governance. Delegates will examine issues of sovereign debt sustainability, fiscal policy coordination across economies, international monetary reform, and the development of equitable financing mechanisms for sustainable development — particularly in the context of developing and emerging market nations.

ECOFIN at SIMATS MUN 2026 demands a high degree of economic literacy, analytical rigour, and the ability to translate macroeconomic data into actionable policy frameworks. Delegates must engage with the policies of institutions such as the IMF, World Bank, and WTO while constructing resolutions that address both short-term fiscal crises and long-term structural economic vulnerabilities in the global South.

Global Finance Debt Restructuring Sustainable Development Fiscal Policy IMF & World Bank

04
AIPPM

All India Political Parties Meet

Agenda Topic

National Security, Internal Sovereignty, and the Legislative Framework for Counterterrorism Policy in India

The All India Political Parties Meet is SIMATS MUN 2026's most intense and politically charged committee — a high-fidelity simulation of India's democratic parliamentary discourse. Delegates assume the roles of representatives from India's diverse political landscape, bringing authentic party positions, ideological standpoints, and legislative strategies to the committee floor.

AIPPM is characterised by dynamic cross-party debate, real-time political interventions, and the necessity of building workable legislative coalitions across ideological divides. Delegates must demonstrate a thorough understanding of Indian constitutional law, parliamentary procedure, current policy debates, and the complex interplay between national security imperatives and civil liberties. This committee rewards preparation, eloquence, and the strategic instincts of a seasoned political negotiator.

Indian Parliament National Security Counterterrorism Legislative Debate Political Strategy

05
IPC

International Press Corps

Mandate

Documenting, Reporting, and Analysing the Proceedings of SIMATS MUN 2026 with Journalistic Excellence

The International Press Corps is the media arm of SIMATS MUN 2026, responsible for the real-time journalistic coverage and editorial documentation of all conference proceedings. IPC delegates operate as professional journalists — reporting from committee rooms, conducting delegate interviews, drafting press releases, and producing digital and written media content that captures the intellectual and diplomatic pulse of the conference.

Unlike traditional committees, IPC delegates do not represent countries — instead, they represent media organisations and journalistic outlets. The IPC provides an unparalleled opportunity for delegates with interests in journalism, communications, public relations, and digital media to develop real-world skills in fast-paced editorial environments. Participants will practise news writing, investigative analysis, photography, and live reporting — building a tangible professional portfolio in the process.

Press Freedom Digital Media Live Reporting Photojournalism Editorial Writing

06
CRISIS

Crisis Committee

Scenario

To Be Revealed at Conference — Strategic Geopolitical & Humanitarian Crisis Simulation

The Crisis Committee is the most dynamic and unpredictable forum at SIMATS MUN 2026 — designed for delegates who thrive under pressure and excel in strategic, adaptive thinking. Unlike conventional committees, the Crisis Committee operates in real time, with evolving scenarios, surprise developments, and breaking updates that fundamentally alter the diplomatic landscape mid-session.

Delegates must respond to unfolding geopolitical crises, humanitarian emergencies, and strategic dilemmas with speed, precision, and diplomatic creativity. The committee format blends elements of crisis management, intelligence briefings, bilateral negotiations, and directive drafting — all against the backdrop of time-sensitive, high-stakes decision-making. This committee is best suited for experienced delegates who possess strong foundational MUN skills and the ability to think beyond conventional diplomatic frameworks.

Crisis Management Geopolitics Strategic Thinking Real-Time Simulation Diplomatic Pressure
How It Works

Committee Structure

A Framework Built for Rigorous Diplomatic Simulation

Executive Board Leadership

Each committee is presided over by a dedicated Executive Board comprising a Chair and Vice-Chair, who manage sessions, maintain procedural order, evaluate delegate performance, and ensure the highest standards of diplomatic discourse.

Country & Role Portfolios

Delegates are assigned specific country portfolios or political party roles prior to the conference. Assignments reflect a balance of global representation and ensure each perspective is meaningfully represented in committee debate.

Rules of Procedure

All committees operate according to a standard set of MUN Rules of Procedure governing speakers lists, moderated and unmoderated caucuses, motion procedures, amendments, and voting mechanisms — ensuring structured, fair, and authentic debate.

Resolutions & Directives

The culmination of each committee session is the drafting, debating, and voting on resolutions or directives. These documents represent the collective diplomatic output of the committee and are evaluated as part of delegate assessment and awards.
